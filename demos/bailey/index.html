Bailey Home Service was founded on a mission: "To glorify God through service to others by having a positive influence on all who come in contact with us, and to treat each person with honor, dignity and respect." That mission drives everything we do — from the precision of our Manual J load calculations to the way we explain your options without pressure.

"To Love your home means to not hurt it. Don't do things to your home that will harm it. I see mistakes all the time that are mostly done because of ignorance. You don't know what you don't know until you know it."

We view your home as a complete system — every part interconnected. A microwave installed wrong can let hot attic air into your kitchen. A bathroom fan vented incorrectly can fill your attic with mold. This is Building Science, and it's the foundation of everything we do.

🔬 The Systemic View

"A systemic view is the view that all systems are composed of interrelated subsystems. A whole is not just the sum of the parts, but the system itself can be explained only as a totality." — Fred Kerlinger. Your HVAC, insulation, ventilation, and building envelope must work as one.

📋 Manual J — The National Standard

Every air conditioning system we design starts with a Manual J load calculation — a national code requirement that most contractors skip. Without it, you get equipment that's too big, too small, or wrong for your home. We do it right, every time.

🌡️ Thermal Imaging Diagnostics

We don't guess. Our infrared thermal scans reveal exactly where heat is escaping, where moisture is hiding, and where your insulation is failing. Basic test: $300. Comprehensive: up to $1,000. Every single home should be tested.

The Technical Authority

20 Years of Knowing What Others Miss

Jon at Bailey Home Service has spent two decades perfecting the science of home performance. Here's the expertise most HVAC companies don't have — but every homeowner deserves.

📐 Duct Work: The Make-or-Break

"Duct work is the most important part of your central heating and air conditioning system. It is also the easiest part to screw up." Most contractors undersize ducts to win bids — a 5-ton AC on 2-ton ducts performs like a 2-ton system. Bailey designs every duct system for the specific equipment and space, sealed and insulated to R-6 (crawl) / R-8 (attic) code.

"You can have a 5 ton air conditioner installed but if the duct work is sized for a 2 ton... your system will only be 2 ton."

🔬 Freon Leak Detection: 3-Step Method

Most companies give up and replace the coil. Bailey uses a 3-step forensic process: (1) Electronic leak detector to locate the general area. (2) Soap bubble test to pinpoint the exact spot. (3) Coil submersion in water — like finding a tire leak. Older copper coils can be soldered and fixed. Repair cost varies; coil replacement can reach $1,000+.

"Freon leaks cause huge heat loads on the compressor... it can damage the compressor if your system ices up."

🧼 AC Coil Cleaning: The Right Way

Two rules: (1) Never let coil cleaner dry on the coil — rinse 100% off. (2) Be gentle with the fins — bent fins destroy airflow. Bailey provides DIY video guides for outdoor condensers, window ACs, indoor evaporators, and blower wheels. When DIY isn't enough, we do it for $100.

"If you accidentally pull the wires out of the fan and can't figure out where they go... you're going to have to pay a service fee."

🏡 Building Science: The Big Picture

Most contractors know equipment, not homes. Bailey understands Building Science — the study of how a home works as a complete system. Microwave vents into the attic? Mold in 3 years. Dirt piled against foundation? Water intrusion. Wrong insulation placement? Rot behind the walls. We find and fix what others miss.
