Why Businesses Miss Government Tenders

The Problem

  • Tenders are scattered across 6+ portals — staff manually check each one daily
  • High-value tenders have 7–14 day windows — discovery delays mean missed deadlines
  • No geo-filtering — teams waste hours reviewing tenders outside their service area
  • EOI drafting takes 2–4 hours per tender — bottleneck for small teams

Our Solution

  • Scans AusTender, VendorPanel, and BCC hourly via RSS + Apify
  • Gemini 2.5 Flash classifies into Tier A/B/C/Reject with confidence scores
  • Deterministic In-Zone Gate — 731 suburbs, 154 postcodes within 50km of Brisbane/Gold Coast
  • Auto-drafts EOI responses with company credentials and outputs to Google Sheets

Under the Hood

The 4 n8n Workflows Powering the System

1

Hourly Tender Scanner

Runs every 60 minutes via cron trigger. Fetches new listings from AusTender RSS, VendorPanel RSS, and Brisbane City Council (via Apify actor). Applies keyword matching against 24 terms (security, cleaning, guard, CCTV, access control, etc.) and deduplicates against previously seen tender IDs stored in n8n static data.

CRON → /tender-scanner
2

AI Classifier (Gemini 2.5 Flash)

Each matched tender is sent to Gemini 2.5 Flash via OpenRouter with a structured prompt. Returns a JSON object with tier (A/B/C/Reject), confidence score, reasoning, and extracted metadata (location, deadline, category). Tier A = direct service match, Tier B = adjacent opportunity, Tier C = subcontracting potential.

POST → /ai-classify
3

In-Zone Gate (Deterministic Geo-Filter)

Zero-cost, zero-API geo-filter. Checks the AI-extracted location against a hardcoded lookup table of 731 QLD suburbs and 154 postcodes within 50km of Brisbane CBD and Gold Coast. Uses string normalization and fuzzy suburb matching. No AI hallucination risk — purely deterministic pass/fail.

IF → /zone-check
4

Bid Draft Builder

For Tier A and B tenders that pass the In-Zone Gate, Gemini drafts a tailored Expression of Interest referencing CGS's licenses, service area, and relevant experience. Output is written to a 19-column Google Sheet with tender URL, classification, deadline, draft EOI, and action status.

POST → /draft-eoi
